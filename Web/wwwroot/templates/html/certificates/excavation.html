Excavation instructions applicable to the Permit Holder who is responsible for assuring compliance
  • When sloping is utilized as a protective measure for excavations up to 6 meters deep, an acceptable sloping is 1.5 horizontal to 1 vertical. Steeper slopes may be allowed where pre defined Country specific tables or charts exist based on soil classification or where recognized soil engineering expertise personnel are involved in defining acceptable steeper slopes.
  • Identify Overhead electrical lines (or other overhead infrastructure) and assure provisions are in place to prevent contact (or encroachment beyond established safe distances when high voltage power lines exist) by equipment.
  • Underground cabling and piping must be clearly marked /identified prior to excavation.
  • Within 0.5 meters (1.6 feet) around piping and cabling, only careful hand probing and digging may be used.
  • Exposed underground piping and cabling to be supported.
  • Soil removed from excavation must be a minimum of 0.75 meters setback from the edge of the excavation.
  • Excavations 6 meters or deeper require the services of a specialized Subject Matter Expert (Engineered credentialed or similarly recognized soils and protective systems expert) on soil stability and controls who is commissioned to develop a safe excavation plan.
  • Contaminated soil requires the use of PPE as well as specific Hazardous waste handling/disposal plans.
  • Utilize confined space entry certificate when work meets the confined space definition.
  • Barriers and signage must be present to prevent unauthorized entry, and warning systems in place for vehicles.
  • A designate authorized person(s) must be identified below for daily inspections (may be Permit Holder).
